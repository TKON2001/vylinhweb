High school students take part in fire rescue drill

01/10/2025

12th-grade students in Hanoi participated in a comprehensive fire rescue drill organized in collaboration with local fire services. The training event, which took place at the city's main fire station, provided students with hands-on experience in emergency response and fire safety.

During the drill, students learned essential skills including:

  • Proper use of different types of fire extinguishers
  • Emergency evacuation procedures and routes
  • Basic first aid techniques for fire-related injuries
  • Communication protocols during emergencies
  • Fire prevention best practices

Fire Chief Nguyen Van Minh emphasized the importance of youth involvement in fire safety: "These students are not just learning to protect themselves, but also becoming community safety ambassadors. The knowledge they gain today could save lives tomorrow."

The drill is part of a broader initiative to enhance emergency preparedness in educational institutions across Vietnam. Following the success of this program, similar drills are planned for other schools in the region.
